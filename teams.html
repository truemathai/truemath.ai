The problem at scale

Your team got faster with AI. Your error rate got faster too.

Every practice leader who deployed AI in the last eighteen months is now living with the same three problems. They are not visible on a dashboard. They show up in the work.

Problem 01
Thirty people, thirty methodologies
Every person on your team prompts the LLM their own way. Same calculation, different assumptions, different answers. The inconsistency does not surface until a client compares two deliverables, or two team members compare notes.
Problem 02
Your senior people are the QA function
The most expensive person in the room is now spending a third of their time double-checking the math in everyone else's work. That is not what you hired them for. It is also the only thing standing between your firm and the wrong number going to a client.
Problem 03
Every new hire reinvents the wheel
A junior joins. They get an LLM seat on day one. They figure out their own way to calculate the things your firm has been calculating for twenty years. Tribal knowledge does not transfer through a chat window.
What changes with TrueMath

One source of calculation truth. Every seat. Every client.

TrueMath sits alongside the LLMs your team already uses. The LLM handles the conversation. TrueMath handles the math. Your team gets the speed of AI and the consistency of a single, governed methodology, without changing the tools they work in.

Your firm's methodology, version-locked
The way your firm calculates things, captured once, governed centrally, deployed to every seat. Junior or senior, week one or year ten, the math comes out the same way every time.
Senior time goes back to senior work
When the math is governed, your senior people stop being the QA function. They review judgment, not arithmetic. The hours you reclaim go back to the work they were hired to do.
New hires productive on day one
Onboarding stops being a six-month transfer of tribal knowledge. The methodology is in the platform. New people use it the same way senior people use it, the day they walk in.
Every output is auditable
When a client questions a number, you can show them where it came from. Formula, version, inputs, timestamp. The audit trail writes itself, on every calculation, every seat, every day.
The choice

Three ways teams handle this. One of them scales.

Most firms are doing the first one and quietly hoping nothing goes wrong. The second is what large firms try when the first stops working. The third is what TrueMath gives you on day one.

Option 1
Spreadsheets and tribal knowledge
Consistency
A different version of the master spreadsheet on every senior's laptop. The newest junior built their own from scratch.
Audit trail
The spreadsheet itself, if you can find the right version. If the senior who built it has left, good luck.
Onboarding
Six months of shadowing. The new hire develops their own habits along the way.
Risk
One missed update propagates silently across every active client deliverable.
Option 2
Build an internal tool
Consistency
Excellent, once it ships. Eighteen months from now. If the project does not get descoped first.
Audit trail
Whatever your engineering team has time to build, maintain, and re-justify on every release.
Onboarding
Solved, eventually. By people who do not understand your methodology as well as the senior person who left last quarter.
Risk
You now own a calculation platform. The maintenance, the bugs, the security review, all of it.
Option 3
TrueMath for Teams
Consistency
Every seat, same methodology, same answer. From the day the team is provisioned.
Audit trail
Built in. Every calculation carries formula, version, inputs, timestamp. Defensible to any client who asks.
Onboarding
Day one. New hire opens the platform, calculates the way the senior person does, with the same assumptions.
Risk
